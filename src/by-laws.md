## Article I: Our Goals and Activities

### Section I. Our Name

The organization is called the "PENINSULA FERMENTATION SOCIETY" (referred to as "PFS").

### Section II. Our Mission

PFS aims to share knowledge of the craft and science of fermenting, build our skills as brewers and fermenters, and foster camaraderie around fermented beverages.

### Section III. What We Do

The club:

a. Hosts monthly meetings to sample beverages, provide feedback, present educational content, and conduct business.

b. Arranges group brewing and fermentation preparation days.

c. Organizes outings and special events for members.

d. Encourages interaction with local brewing-related groups and businesses.

e. Promotes participation in homebrew competitions.

### Section IV. Who We Are

PFS operates as a 501(c)(7) social club with voluntary membership. The organization welcomes individuals engaged in craft brewing as a hobby or interested in learning about fermentation.

## Article II: Our Members

### Section I. Non-Discrimination Clause

PFS prohibits discrimination based on race, sex, color, national origin, disability, religion, creed, ancestry, veteran status, marital or parental status, sexual orientation, or gender identity.

### Section II. Regular Membership

a. Members must be of legal drinking age in Washington and engaged in or interested in legal home fermentation production. Members receive newsletter access, social media group membership, property use privileges, meeting attendance rights, and voting privileges.

b. Membership requires paying annual dues, prorated by months remaining in the calendar year.

c. Officers may review memberships if conduct interferes with the club mission and may revoke membership, with prorated refunds issued.

### Section III. Guest Membership

a. Guests have no voting rights and may attend up to two monthly meetings. After two meetings, attendance requires establishing regular membership.

b. Guests must sign liability waivers before each meeting or event.

c. Spouses and significant others are non-voting unless they hold active memberships with filed waivers.

d. Neighboring club board members may receive extended guest access upon Executive Committee approval, reviewed monthly.

### Section IV. New Membership

a. New members undergo a three-month trial period during which they may:

1. Opt out and receive prorated refunds based on full months remaining in the calendar year (current month excluded).
2. Have their suitability reviewed by officers regarding genuine hobby interest and respectful conduct.

b. Officers may decline membership applications but cannot deny membership solely based on the reasons identified in Article II Section I.

c. Officers may declare a membership moratorium if deemed necessary, consulting members at large first.

### Section V. Member Rights and Responsibilities

a. All paid members may vote on matters requiring general membership votes.

b. Members may seek reimbursement for reasonable PFS activity expenses with prior officer approval.

c. All members are expected to contribute in some way each year to the club's mission.

d. Only voting members may participate in internal competitions, though non-voting members may judge.

### Section VI. Release of Liability

a. Club participation is entirely voluntary for members and guests.

b. Club activities may include alcohol consumption, which can affect perception, judgment, and reaction time.

c. Members and guests implicitly accept responsibility for personal choices and conduct, agreeing to hold the club collectively and the officers individually harmless regarding alcohol consumption outcomes.

## Article III: Club Dues

### Section I. Amount, Payment, and Use

a. Annual membership dues are $30.00, collected in January from renewing members.

b. Members receive a two-month grace period after expiration to renew; failure to renew results in loss of voting status until dues are paid.

c. Dues fund PFS activities, food and beverages at meetings, liability insurance, competition awards, member gifts, and promotional materials or apparel.

d. The dollar amount of dues may be changed up to once annually, as determined by the executive committee, and approved by at least fifty percent of members in good standing.

## Article IV: Governance

### Section I. Administration

a. Elected PFS officers control all administrative powers unless the Executive Committee delegates authority to other members in good standing.

b. No PFS Officer or member of the Board shall receive compensation for any service provided to PFS. The club may provide compensation and expense reimbursement for non-voting positions.

c. Officers may reimburse members for reasonably incurred expenses in performing duties.

### Section II. Executive Committee

a. The Executive Committee consists of three to seven elected board members, plus two permanent founding members (identified in Appendix 1) who retain voting status until relinquishing duties.

Required positions: **President**, **Treasurer**, **Secretary**. Additional positions: **Vice President**, **Grand Hydrometer**, **Event Coordinator**, **Communications Director**.

b. The Board may establish new positions to support future expansion.

c. The President resolves tied committee votes.

### Section III. Club Officer Qualifications

a. Officers must be of legal drinking age in Washington, active voting members in good standing, and engaged in home fermentation science.

b. Except for initial elections, candidates must have eleven months of membership before holding office.

### Section IV. Officer Selection Procedures, Terms, and Process

a. Any PFS member in good standing willing to fulfill office duties may fill a position.

b. Officer terms last one calendar year, beginning January 1st. No term limits apply.

c. Members must self-nominate. Individuals may nominate themselves for multiple positions but may hold only one if elected to several.

d. Nominations finalize in November; voting completes by December. Candidates must renew membership for the next year when nominated.

e. All members are eligible to vote within the method(s) and forum(s) designated yearly by officers or PFS consensus.

### Section V. Resignation of Officers

a. Officers may resign by written notice to the President or Vice-President via delivery, mail, or email, taking effect immediately upon receipt.

b. Vacancies occurring during a yearly term may be filled through soliciting volunteers and conducting a membership vote if necessary.

### Section VI. Club Officer Duties and Responsibilities

**a. President (Primary Fermenter)**

1. Serves as Board Chairman.
2. Presides at membership and Board meetings.
3. Appoints ad hoc committee chairs and members.
4. Regularly reviews financial records.
5. Upholds club by-laws.
6. Plans and prepares meeting agendas for membership and Board.
7. Calls meetings to order and conducts them orderly.
8. Oversees election conduct and counting.
9. Acts as primary liaison with the American Homebrewers Association, Washington Homebrewers Association, and related organizations.

**b. Vice President (Secondary Fermenter)**

1. Presides at meetings when the President is absent.
2. Assists the President and performs their duties as needed.
3. Maintains and updates member name badges.
4. Oversees purchase and distribution of merchandise, apparel, and promotional materials.
5. Collaborates with officers planning and executing club activities.
6. Assists in preparing meeting agendas.

**c. Treasurer (Keeper of Cash)**

1. Maintains membership records including names and contact information.
2. Manages club funds: collects dues, notifies delinquent members, maintains accounts, disburses reimbursements, and provides financial reports.
3. Oversees club liability insurance through the American Homebrewers Association.
4. Submits financial condition reports at board meetings and upon request; prepares the annual budget for board approval before annual elections and membership presentation.
5. Ensures check signing authority for the President, founders, and Treasurer.
6. Files required tax returns and maintains non-profit status.
7. Collaborates with officers on club activities.

**d. Secretary (Scribe)**

1. Records and maintains meeting minutes; designates others to record when unavailable.
2. Publishes minutes in a member-accessible forum.
3. Chairs meetings if both the President and Vice President are absent.
4. Collaborates with officers on club activities.
5. Monitors waiver of liability status for guests before meetings or events.

**e. Grand Hydrometer**

1. Coordinates and hosts monthly "Brew-To Style" challenges during meetings.
2. Assists the President coordinating workshops and demonstrations.
3. Aligns Brew-To-Style challenges with local breweries willing to brew winning entries when possible.

**f. Events Coordinator**

1. Recruits volunteers for club-supported events, including booth staffing and festival beer pouring.
2. Coordinates food for all meetings and events; manages the chair's budget throughout the year.
3. Maintains a record of all property owned by the club, supervises its proper use and safekeeping, and maintains the required inventory of supplies and equipment.

**g. Communications Director**

1. Serves as primary contact for incoming emails and social media, with authority to delegate action items.
2. Communicates club news through the monthly newsletter, monthly meeting announcements, periodic blast emails to the membership, and updates posted to social media sites.

### Section VII. Committees

a. The Board authorizes committees (other than nominating) and defines their powers and duties.

b. The President appoints committees subject to officer confirmation.

c. No committee, officer, or member may contract debts rendering the association liable unless Board-approved; previously authorized committees may direct the Treasurer to meet current expenses.

d. The Board or President must establish committee goals.

e. The Board or President may dissolve committees, reappoint them, or replace committee chairs.

f. Committee members must update the club monthly on progress toward established goals.

## Article V: Quorums

### Section I. Voting Quorums

a. A membership voting quorum requires no fewer than one-third of active membership as reported by the Treasurer at the previous Board meeting.

b. An Executive Committee quorum consists of one-half the Executive Committee plus one.

## Article VI: Amendment of By-Laws

### Section I. Petitioning for and Enacting By-Law Modification

a. Any member may petition for by-law modifications by notifying an officer or introducing proposals at club meetings. A proposed change will be voted on at a club meeting and/or via alternate forum(s) available to all members until an eighty percent majority determines approval or rejection.

b. Exceptions include:

1. Annual updates to Appendix 1's past officers list by the Club Secretary in January.
2. Release of Liability modifications (Appendix 2) made by Club Officers as needed for law changes, requiring only one-half club membership concurrence.

## Article VII: Dissolution of Club

### Section I. Dissolution of Club

a. Any member may motion for dissolution, affirmed by fifty percent of present membership at a scheduled meeting. The Executive Committee then notifies all active members of the pending vote.

b. The dissolution vote occurs at the next scheduled meeting, conducted in person only; no accommodations for absent members apply.

c. The club may only be dissolved upon the affirmation of at least 90 percent of the active membership, or with no more than one dissenter, whichever number is greater. If insufficient members attend, the vote fails.

d. Dissolution votes cannot occur more than once per twelve-month period.

### Section II. Liquidation of Club-Owned Assets and Funds

a. Upon a favorable dissolution vote, all club assets are sold at fair market value determined by the Executive Committee, with proceeds consolidated into PFS holdings.

b. After returning prorated dues, remaining funds are evenly dispersed among the active membership pool based upon the total number of months of active membership.
